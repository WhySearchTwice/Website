Privacy Policy

Last Modified: June 7th, 2013

The nature of Retrospect requires that you grant our extension access to your web browsing history. The information shared with us is transmitted to our servers to facilitate synchronization between your devices and enable you to search your history quickly. No information that we gather will ever be given to third parties (not even with your permission!)

What we collect:

After installing the Chrome extension, you will be prompted to grant us access to your email address. This email address will be used solely as a method of uniquely identifying you and correlating your multiple devices into a single account. We may use this email address in the future to contact you about our services however it will never be sold or used for advertising.

For the duration that you have our extension installed and activated, Chrome will report your web browsing history to our extension which will store this information on our server and synchronize to your other devices. Information included in these messages is listed below:

  • The IDs given to a tab and window by Chrome
  • The date and time that a tab is opened and closed
  • The web address that a tab accessed

We will never (and cannot) collect information from “incognito” sessions unless you explicitly give our extension permission to run in this mode.

How we use information we collect:

The information collected is used to display your browsing history in your timeline. This information is stored on our servers to allow it to be quickly searched and is sent to your devices whenever a search is performed.

We periodically create aggregate calculations of the number of times pages were visited for statistical purposes. This information is completely anonymized before being created.
